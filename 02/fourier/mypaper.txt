B. Frequency Domain Learning  
The Fourier Transform (FT) is a fundamental technique in frequency domain analysis and has been applied across various computer vision tasks in recent years. Early methods primarily focused on feature extraction in the frequency domain, using FT to convert between spatial and frequency domains. Due to the efficiency of the Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) algorithm, FT-based approaches have emerged as effective token mixers and efficient alternatives to the self-attention mechanism. For example, DFFormer [13] utilizes the dynamic weighted filters as the token mixer. Some studies have incorporated FT into medical image segmentation. EM-Net [14] applies learnable weight parameters to frequency features for efficient frequency domain learning. Additionally, more recent methods have extended FT’s application by performing filtering operations in the frequency domain. FE-VAD [15] decouples the temporal information from the video stream into high- and low-frequency components, which are subsequently enhanced through separate high and low frequency filters. Our AFB is closely related to frequency filtering methods. Unlike previous approaches that specify a fixed filter type, our filters in AFB can dynamically transform into high-pass, low-pass or band-pass based on the input features, those to be filtered. This flexibility allows the preservation of high-frequency boundary detail while maintaining low-frequency semantic features
