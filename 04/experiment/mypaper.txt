C. Comparison Experiments  
Comparison with State-of-the-arts: We compare the SAFMamba with nine state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods, including CNN-based , transformer-based, and Mamba-based methods. The results of different methods on the ACDC dataset are shown in Table I. Our SAF-Mamba achieves the highest average DSC of 92.21%, 
surpassing the second-place model, Hiformer, by 0.39%. 
It outperforms other methods by a significant margin in RV segmentation, where the DSC reaches 90.87%.
 This demonstrates that our approach effectively captures the size variations in cardiac substructures. SAF-Mamba also excels in HD95 metrics, reporting a score of 1.05. This indicates that the AFBs in our method preserve critical detailed information in feature maps. However, as the myocardium has fewer annotations compared to the ventricles, our method did not achieve the best DSC for MYO segmentation, suggesting that it struggles to effectively handle the class imbalance. The M&Ms dataset, which comes from multiple clinical centers and different scanner vendors, presents greater segmentation challenges due to the inhomogeneous imaging protocols used across these centers. As a result, the segmentation performance shown in Table II is lower compared to the ACDC dataset. However, our SAF-Mamba still outperforms  the nine other methods in terms of both average DSC and HD95 metrics, with a DSC of 88.43%
  and a HD95 of 12.14, respectively. Notably, SAF-Mamba achieves the best performance across Vendors A, B, C, and D, demonstrating its ability to effectively address the challenge of variation across multiple centers and scanner vendors in the M&Ms dataset, highlighting the strong generalization capability of our model.
